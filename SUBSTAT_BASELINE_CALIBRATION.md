This document establishes a **C-tier baseline** representing an average person (50th percentile) for each substat. From this anchor point, we then establish realistic ranges and difficulty curves to ensure:

- **Some substats are achievable** (Easy path to B/A tier)
- **Some substats are challenging** (Hard to reach S/SS)

## Difficulty Tiers

### EASY TO ACHIEVE (Achievable B/A for most)
These substats have a **shallow curve** – easier to improve from C to B/A:
- **Adherence** (diet discipline easy to claim)
- **Expression** (communication learnable)
- **Empathy** (basic kindness accessible)
- **Speed** (natural for some, trainable for others)
- **Perception** (pattern recognition learnable)

**Why:** These are skills people can develop with effort, or traits that don't require deep change.

---

### MODERATE DIFFICULTY (B-tier achievable, A-tier challenging)
These substats have a **normal curve** – moderate effort needed:
- **Strength** (requires consistent training)
- **Reason** (logic improves with practice)
- **Knowledge** (requires study)
- **Dexterity** (deliberate practice needed)
- **Balance** (requires lifestyle adjustment)
- **Adaptability** (requires openness)

**Why:** These require sustained effort but aren't dramatically harder.

---

### HARD TO ACHIEVE (A/S-tier rare)
These substats have a **steep curve** – difficulty exponentially increases:
- **Willpower** (few have true self-discipline)
- **Purpose** (requires self-discovery)
- **Composure** (requires years of training)
- **Conviction** (requires standing alone)
- **Focus** (ADHD/distraction culture makes this rare)
- **Tranquility** (modern life is stressful)
- **Resilience** (requires overcoming repeated failures)

**Why:** These require deep personal work, life experience, or unusual discipline.

---

### CULTURAL/PERSONAL VARIATION (Wide Range)
These substats vary **dramatically** by person:
- **Faith** (5-95%: atheist to deeply religious)
- **Charisma** (20-95%: introverted to naturally charming)
- **Vision** (25-80%: some naturally future-focused, others present-focused)
- **Style** (25-90%: cultural and personal preference)
- **Imagination** (15-95%: creative minds vs. literal thinkers)

**Why:** No universal baseline; depends on worldview/personality.

## Percentile Distribution Guide

### How to Convert Raw Score → Percentile

For **tests with clear metrics** (e.g., bench press, typing speed):

```
percentile = (raw_score - min_value) / (max_value - min_value) * 100

Examples:
- Bench press: (60 kg - 20 kg) / (200 kg - 20 kg) * 100 = ~22% (below average)
- 5k run: (28 min - 16 min) / (50 min - 16 min) * 100 = ~35% (below average)
- Typing: (40 WPM - 10 WPM) / (120 WPM - 10 WPM) * 100 = ~27% (below average)
```

For **subjective tests** (sliders, questionnaires):
- Direct mapping: 0-100 scale = 0-100 percentile

For **mini-games** (Simon Says level):
```
percentile = (level_reached - min_level) / (max_level - min_level) * 100

Simon Says example:
- C-tier at level 12-13 of 25
- percentile = (12.5 - 1) / (25 - 1) * 100 = ~48% ≈ 50% (C-tier)
```

## Inflation vs. Deflation Strategy

### Intentionally EASY (Shallow Curve)
These can reach A-tier relatively easily:
- **Adherence** (diet tracking → A)
- **Expression** (good writing → A)
- **Empathy** (kindness → A)

**Rationale:** These feel achievable, motivating players to try.

---

### Intentionally HARD (Steep Curve)
These are difficult to reach S-tier:
- **Willpower** (requires extreme discipline)
- **Focus** (modern ADHD prevalence)
- **Composure** (years of training)
- **Purpose** (deep self-discovery)
- **Tranquility** (stress is universal)

**Rationale:** These feel earned, making S-tier meaningful.

---

### BALANCED (Normal Curve)
These follow standard distribution:
- **Strength** (effort → results)
- **Knowledge** (study → learning)
- **Reason** (practice → improvement)

**Rationale:** Standard progression feels fair.

---

### WIDE VARIATION (Personal)
These have no "right" answer:
- **Faith** (5-95% depending on belief)
- **Charisma** (varies by personality)
- **Vision** (varies by worldview)
- **Imagination** (varies by cognitive style)

**Rationale:** No gatekeeping; personal authenticity celebrated.
