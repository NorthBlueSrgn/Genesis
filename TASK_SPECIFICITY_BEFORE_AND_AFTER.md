# Task Specificity: Before & After Comparison

## Visual Examples of Improvements

### Physical Fitness Domain

#### ❌ BEFORE (Generic & Vague)
```
Novice:
  - "Mechanical Symmetry: 3 sets of 12 reps focusing on form and eccentric control."
  - "Aerobic Base: 20m LISS session. Target Heart Rate Zone 2."
  - Weekly: "Single set of maximum effort (reps) on a core mechanical movement."

Intermediate:
  - "Volume Loading: 5 sets of 8 reps at 70% intensity. Log metrics for every set."
  - "Kinetic Isolate: 30m intensive practice on your weakest mechanical link."
  - Weekly: "90m high-volume session. Total tonnage target must exceed daily avg by 25%."

Advanced:
  - "Intensive Output: 90m high-intensity session. Push to RPE 9 on final sequences."
  - "Kinetic Audit: Video analysis of 3 peak sets. Identify and resolve power leaks."
  - Weekly: "Attempt a new 3RM (3-Rep Max) on a primary specialization movement."

Master:
  - "Apex Feat: Attempt a personal best or enter elite level competitive testing."
  - "Strategic Innovation: Design and execute a 4-week custom peak-performance block."
  - Weekly: "Complete a 3-hour kinetic gauntlet or multi-phase endurance event."
```

**Problems:**
- "Focusing on form" - HOW? What specifically?
- "Power leaks" - What does that mean?
- "Peak-performance block" - What metrics?
- No measurable success criteria

---

#### ✅ AFTER (Specific & Actionable)
```
Novice:
  - "Form Foundation: 3 sets of 12 reps on primary lift with video review. 
     Focus on 5-second negatives."
  - "Cardio Baseline: 25m Zone 2 (conversational pace). Maintain HR 120-140. 
     Log distance/pace."
  - Weekly: "Perform 1RM test on squat/bench/deadlift. Record max weight achieved."

Intermediate:
  - "Volume Circuit: 5×8@75% on main lift + 4 accessories. Track RPE for each set. 
     Total session >45m."
  - "Weakness Protocol: 30m isolated work on your lowest lift. 3 sets of 10 reps 
     with pauses. Log weakness metrics."
  - Weekly: "90m continuous strength session. Total tonnage must be 25% higher 
     than daily average."

Advanced:
  - "Peak Performance: 90m compound + accessory work at RPE 8-9. 4-5 main sets, 
     push near-maximal effort."
  - "Biomechanics Deep Dive: Video record 3 max-effort sets. Analyze bar path, 
     knee drive, hip mechanics. Document 3 cues."
  - Weekly: "Test a new 1RM or perform a heavy double (2RM) on primary lift. 
     Must exceed previous baseline."

Master:
  - "Elite Competition: Enter a local meet, set a personal record, or achieve 
     top-5 lift in your category."
  - "Periodization Mastery: Architect a 12-week peak cycle with macrocycles, 
     mesocycles, and periodized deloads."
  - Weekly: "2-3 hour multi-lift gauntlet. Complete max-effort sequences on 3+ 
     lifts with minimal rest."
```

**Improvements:**
- ✓ Specific metrics (5-second negatives, HR 120-140, RPE 8-9, 3 cues)
- ✓ Clear success criteria (Log, Record, Document, Exceed)
- ✓ Progressive difficulty evident
- ✓ Measurable outcomes (tonnage ↑25%, 1RM test, top-5 achievement)

### Technical (Programming) Domain

#### ❌ BEFORE
```
Novice:
  - "15m Mechanical fundamental conditioning."
  - "Document 3 technical findings in your system log."
  - Weekly: "Review daily logs and identify 1 repeatable success."

Intermediate:
  - "40m Intensive drill session on your primary weak point."
  - "Methodology Audit: Deconstruct one expert protocol and apply it."
  - Weekly: "Perform a 2-hour 'Hard Block' session of uninterrupted work."

Advanced:
  - "2-hour Elite application of domain-specific skills."
  - "Efficiency Strip: Isolate and solve a 1% drag in your current workflow."
  - Weekly: "Publish or submit a finished piece of work or achievement."

Master:
  - "Submit finished work or enter elite competition."
  - "Redefine your personal baseline for 'Excellence'."
  - Weekly: "Create a piece of documentation or content that aids others."
```

**Problems:**
- "Mechanical fundamental conditioning" - WAT?
- "Methodology Audit" - for coding? Too vague
- "Elite application" - of what?
- Could mean anything for any domain

---

#### ✅ AFTER
```
Novice:
  - "Code Foundations: Complete 1 tutorial or documentation section (30m). 
     Build 1 simple project. Document what you learned."
  - "Bug Squashing: Debug and fix 5 beginner coding problems. Trace the issue, 
     understand the solution, move on."
  - Weekly: "Create a functional small project (calculator, to-do list, etc). 
     Test all features. Commit code."

Intermediate:
  - "60m coding sprint: Build 2-3 features for a medium project. Use proper 
     design patterns. Code review your own work."
  - "System Design Study: Analyze 1 open-source project. Document architecture, 
     key components, and 3 design decisions."
  - Weekly: "Refactor a feature for performance. Measure speed improvement 
     (profiling/benchmarking). Document gains."

Advanced:
  - "2-hour architectural session: Design and implement a complex feature or 
     microservice. Optimize for scale/performance."
  - "Debugging Mastery: Tackle a difficult bug in production-like code. Use 
     profilers/logs. Document root cause and solution."
  - Weekly: "Contribute to open-source, publish a library, or complete a 
     significant architectural overhaul."

Master:
  - "System Design Innovation: Architect a novel system that solves a hard 
     technical problem in production environment."
  - "Leadership Through Code: Mentor junior developers, lead a technical RFC, 
     or influence architectural decisions for large systems."
  - Weekly: "Publish a deep technical blog post, speak at a conference, or 
     release major open-source contribution."
```

**Improvements:**
- ✓ Programming-specific (code review, profiling, RFC, microservices)
- ✓ Clear deliverables (Build project, Refactor, Document, Contribute)
- ✓ Measurable progress (5 bugs, 3 features, profiling metrics)
- ✓ Natural progression from beginner to recognized expert

#### ✅ AFTER
```

  - "Daily Studio Time: 30m creative practice (writing, music, art). Complete 
     1 small work or exploration. No pressure for quality."
  - "Inspiration Mining: Study 3 works by artists you admire. Document 
     1 technique, 1 theme, 1 emotion you'd like to capture."
  - Weekly: "Create 1 finished creative work. Record it (audio), photograph it 
     (visual), or finalize it. Share for feedback."

Intermediate:
  - "60m creative sprint: Compose/write/paint 1 medium-length work. Push 
     emotional depth. Iterate based on self-critique."
  - "Deconstruction Session: Analyze 1 master work. What makes it resonate? 
     Document story arc, color theory, lyrical density, etc."
  - Weekly: "Finish a creative piece. Get feedback from 2+ people. Make 1 major 
     revision. Share publicly or perform."

Advanced:
  - "2-hour intensive creation: Produce advanced work (EP, painting series, 
     screenplay draft). Push thematic/technical boundaries."
  - "Experimental Fusion: Blend 2+ creative disciplines or genres. Create 
     hybrid work. Document what you discovered."
  - Weekly: "Release/exhibit work (album, show, publication). Receive 10+ 
     positive responses or measurable reach."

Master:
  - "Signature Collection: Create a cohesive body of work (10+ pieces) with 
     distinct voice, theme, and technical mastery."
  - "Creative Mentorship: Teach workshops, publish a guide, or mentor emerging 
     artists. Elevate your creative community."
  - Weekly: "Win awards, sign publishing deals, get major platform feature, or 
     create work with cultural impact."
```

**Improvements:**
- ✓ Creative-specific language (studio, sketch, compose, resonate, performance)
- ✓ Actual creative output (write, paint, compose, EP, album)
- ✓ Feedback mechanisms (share, perform, get feedback)
- ✓ Progression shows real growth (from practice → mastery → industry impact)
